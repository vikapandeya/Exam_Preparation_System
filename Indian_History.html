📅 History at a Glance — Timeline
2500 BC
Indus Valley Civilization (Harappan)
Mature phase begins. Major cities: Mohenjodaro (Sind, Pakistan), Harappa (Punjab, Pakistan), Lothal (Gujarat), Kalibangan (Rajasthan), Dholavira (Gujarat). Town planning, drainage, standardised weights.
1500 BC
Vedic Age Begins
Aryans settle in the Indo-Gangetic plain. Rig Veda composed (oldest text). Early Vedic → Later Vedic (1000–600 BC): Upanishads, Mahabharata, Ramayana composition period.
563 BC
Birth of Gautama Buddha (Lumbini, Nepal)
Siddhartha Gautama → The Buddha. Attained enlightenment at Bodh Gaya (Bihar); gave first sermon at Sarnath (Deer Park). Mahaparinirvana at Kushinagar, 483 BC.
321 BC
Mauryan Empire Founded — Chandragupta Maurya
Chandragupta Maurya (guided by Chanakya/Kautilya) overthrew Nanda dynasty. Established India's first large empire. Arthashastra written by Kautilya. Capital: Pataliputra (Patna).
269 BC
Ashoka the Great ascends throne
Kalinga War (261 BC) → Ashoka embraced Buddhism. Spread Buddhism across Asia; Dhamma. Built Pillars (Lions — now National Emblem). Sent missions to Sri Lanka, Afghanistan, Egypt.
319 AD
Gupta Empire — Golden Age of India
Chandragupta I (319) → Samudragupta → Chandragupta II (Vikramaditya). Nalanda University, Ajanta caves, Aryabhatta (zero, π), Kalidasa (Abhijnanasakuntalam), Varahamihira.
712 AD
Arab Invasion — Muhammad bin Qasim conquers Sind
First Arab/Muslim incursion into Indian subcontinent; Sind & Southern Punjab. Not consolidated; limited impact on mainland India.
1192 AD
Second Battle of Tarain — Muhammad Ghori defeats Prithviraj Chauhan
Prithviraj Chauhan defeated (had won First Battle of Tarain 1191); beginning of Turkish rule in Delhi. Qutb-ud-din Aibak → first Delhi Sultanate ruler.
1206 AD
Delhi Sultanate begins — Slave Dynasty (Mamluk)
Five dynasties: Slave (1206–1290), Khilji (1290–1320), Tughlaq (1320–1414), Sayyid (1414–1451), Lodi (1451–1526). Qutb Minar construction started by Qutb-ud-din Aibak.
1526 AD
First Battle of Panipat — Babur defeats Ibrahim Lodi
Babur (Timur + Genghis Khan descendant) used gunpowder & artillery; defeated Lodi sultanate. Founded Mughal Empire. Baburnama — his autobiography.
1556 AD
Second Battle of Panipat — Akbar defeats Hemu
Akbar (13 years) — under Bairam Khan's regency. Defeat of Hemu (Hindu general of Adil Shah Suri). Akbar's reign (1556–1605) — golden era of Mughal rule; Din-i-Ilahi, Fatehpur Sikri.
1600 AD
East India Company established (Dec 31, 1600)
Queen Elizabeth I granted charter to English East India Company. First factory at Surat (1612). Gradually acquired political power.
1757 AD
Battle of Plassey — British power consolidated in Bengal
Robert Clive defeats Siraj-ud-Daulah (Bengal Nawab); treachery by Mir Jafar. British acquire Diwani rights in Bengal (1765) after Battle of Buxar (1764).
1857 AD
Revolt of 1857 — First War of Independence
Meerut (May 10) → Delhi; Mangal Pandey, Rani Lakshmibai (Jhansi), Tantia Tope, Nana Sahib, Kunwar Singh. Started over greased cartridges (Enfield rifle). Suppressed by end of 1858.
1885 AD
Indian National Congress Founded
A.O. Hume (retired ICS officer), Dadabhai Naoroji, Dinshaw Wacha. First session in Bombay (1885); W.C. Bonnerjee — first President. Moderate phase until 1905; then extremist phase.
1919 AD
Jallianwala Bagh Massacre (April 13, 1919)
General Dyer ordered firing on peaceful gathering (Baisakhi) in Amritsar. 379+ killed (official), thousands injured. Rabindranath Tagore returned knighthood in protest.
1920 AD
Non-Cooperation Movement — Gandhi
Boycott of schools, courts, councils, foreign goods. Suspended after Chauri Chaura incident (Feb 1922) — mob burned police station, 22 policemen killed.
1930 AD
Salt Satyagraha / Dandi March
Gandhi walked 241 miles (Sabarmati → Dandi, Gujarat) from March 12 to April 5. Made salt from sea water in violation of salt laws → Civil Disobedience Movement launched.
1942 AD
Quit India Movement (August 8, 1942)
"Do or Die" — Gandhi at Gowalia Tank Maidan, Bombay. AICC resolution passed. Immediate arrest of all major Congress leaders. Underground movement; most violent phase of freedom struggle.
1947
Independence — August 15, 1947
Mountbatten Plan / Indian Independence Act 1947. Jawaharlal Nehru's "Tryst with Destiny" speech. Partition into India and Pakistan. Integration of 562 princely states — Sardar Patel (Iron Man).

Characteristics of Indus Valley Civilization

  • Discovered by: John Marshall, R.D. Banerji (Mohenjodaro), Daya Ram Sahni (Harappa), 1920s
  • Script: Pictographic; not yet fully deciphered; written right to left (boustrophedon)
  • Town Planning: Grid pattern; underground drainage (most advanced of ancient world); burnt bricks
  • Economy: Agriculture (wheat, barley, cotton), trade with Mesopotamia (Sumer), crafts
  • Religion: Mother Goddess worship, Pashupati (proto-Shiva), tree worship, animal worship
  • Decline theories: Aryan invasion (outdated), climate change, flood, drying of Ghaggar-Hakra (Saraswati) river

Ashoka's Dhamma (Moral Code)

  • Non-violence (Ahimsa), respect for all life
  • Religious tolerance — respect all sects
  • Obedience to parents and elders
  • Liberality (dana) to priests and Brahmanas
  • Officials (Dhamma Mahamatras) appointed to spread Dhamma
  • Sent missions to Sri Lanka (son Mahendra, daughter Sanghamitra)
📌 National Emblem of India — adopted from Sarnath Lion Capital of Ashoka. Four lions (only three visible), Ashoka Chakra (24-spoke wheel) on national flag.

Iron Pillar of Delhi (Mehrauli) — Exam Facts

  • Erected during Chandragupta II (Vikramaditya period), ~4th–5th century AD
  • Height: 7.21 metres; weight: ~6 tonnes; 98% pure wrought iron
  • Remarkably rust-free for 1600+ years — due to high phosphorus content forming a protective layer
  • Inscription in Sanskrit (Gupta Brahmi script) mentions King Chandra, identified as Chandragupta II
  • Originally at a Vishnu temple; later moved to Quwwat-ul-Islam mosque complex by Qutb-ud-din Aibak
📌 Nalanda University: Founded by Kumaragupta I (5th century AD); near Rajgir, Bihar; 10,000+ students from across Asia; 9 million manuscripts; destroyed by Bakhtiyar Khilji (~1193 AD). Hieun Tsang (Xuanzang) — Chinese pilgrim visited during Harsha's reign and studied here 5 years; his account "Si-Yu-Ki" is crucial historical source.

Notable Rulers in Detail

RulerNotable Administration / Legacy
Iltutmish (1211–1236)First to issue silver tanka & copper jital coins; organised iqta system (land grants); real consolidator of Delhi Sultanate; completed Qutb Minar; received Caliphal recognition from Baghdad
Razia Sultana (1236–1240)First woman ruler of Delhi; appointed Malik Jamaluddin Yaqut (Abyssinian) — resented by Turkish nobles; wore no veil, held open durbar; deposed and killed by nobles

Alauddin Khilji (1296–1316)4 market reforms (grain, cloth, cattle & horses, slaves); Diwan-i-Riyasat; paid army in cash not iqta; spy system (barids) at every market; repelled Mongols 4 times; Malik Kafur's Deccan campaigns extended empire south
Muhammad bin Tughlaq (1325–1351)Transfer of capital Delhi → Daulatabad 1327 (forced mass migration); token currency failure (brass/copper coins forged by public); taxation on Doab during famine; eccentric but highly educated (Persian, astronomy, medicine); Ibn Batuta came to his court
Firuz Shah Tughlaq (1351–1388)Welfare state — hospitals (bimaristans), canals (Yamuna to Hissar), gardens; prohibited cruel punishments; taxed Brahmins Jizya (first time); 300 new towns; promoted public works; lenient but weakened state
📌 Qutb Minar: Started by Qutb-ud-din Aibak (1193); completed by Iltutmish; repaired/modified by Firuz Shah Tughlaq. 72.5 m tall; UNESCO World Heritage Site. 5 storeys; dedicated to Sufi saint Qutbuddin Bakhtiyar Kaki. Alai Minar (planned taller — abandoned after Alauddin's death). Alai Darwaza = most beautiful of the 4 gateways.

SystemDetails
Mansabdari SystemMilitary-civil rank system; every official had two ranks — Zat (personal rank/pay grade) and Sawar (cavalry to be maintained); 33 grades from 10 to 10,000; integrated Rajputs & other non-Muslims

🔥 Revolt of 1857

Causes of the Revolt of 1857

CategoryCauseDetails
PoliticalDoctrine of LapseLord Dalhousie's policy — annexed states with no male heir: Satara (1848), Jaitpur & Sambhalpur (1849), Jhansi (1853), Nagpur (1854), Awadh/Oudh (1856) — outraged Indian rulers and their retainers
PoliticalSubsidiary AllianceWellesley's policy — Indian rulers lost sovereignty, maintained British troops at their cost; resentment among princes
EconomicDrain of WealthHeavy land revenue; displacement of artisans by machine-made British goods; Indian weavers and craftsmen ruined; economic exploitation fed into widespread anger
MilitaryEnfield Rifle CartridgesNew Enfield rifle required biting greased cartridges — rumoured to be sealed with cow fat (offensive to Hindus) and pig fat (offensive to Muslims); General Service Enlistment Act (1856) required soldiers to serve overseas, crossing "kala pani" (black water) was religiously forbidden
Social/ReligiousFear of Forced ConversionWidow Remarriage Act (1856), ban on Sati (1829), missionaries allowed (1813) — seen as British interference in religion; fear of conversion to Christianity spread panic among all communities

Centres & Leaders of the Revolt

CentreLeader(s)Key Event
MeerutSepoys of 3rd cavalry + civiliansMay 10, 1857 — first outbreak; sepoys refused greased cartridges, released 85 imprisoned comrades, marched to Delhi
DelhiBahadur Shah Zafar II (last Mughal)Proclaimed Emperor of India by rebels; symbolic centre of revolt; recaptured by British September 1857; Zafar exiled to Rangoon (Burma)
Kanpur (Cawnpore)Nana Sahib + Tantia TopeNana Sahib (adopted son of last Peshwa Bajirao II) led revolt; Bibi Ghar massacre; Tantia Tope continued guerrilla warfare until 1859
LucknowBegum Hazrat MahalWife of deposed Nawab Wajid Ali Shah of Awadh; organised resistance; siege of Lucknow Residency lasted 5 months

Arrah / Jagdishpur (Bihar)Kunwar SinghLed revolt at approximately 80 years of age; brilliant guerrilla tactics; held out longest among leaders; died May 26, 1858 — 2 days after crossing Ganga
BareillyKhan Bahadur KhanNawab of Bareilly; proclaimed governor of Rohilkhand; organised revolt in western UP; later hanged by British
📌 Revolt of 1857 — Names: Called "Sepoy Mutiny" by British; "First War of Indian Independence" by V.D. Savarkar (1909 book). Aftermath: GoI Act 1858 — Crown rule replaced EIC; Governor General became Viceroy; Queen Victoria's Proclamation promised non-interference in religion, equal treatment; but also sowed seeds of divide-and-rule (Morley-Minto communal electorate 1909). Punjab, Bengal, Hyderabad, Rajputana largely unaffected — native princes helped British suppress the revolt.

📅 1947 — Road to Independence

Key Events Leading to Independence

Date/EventDetail
Attlee's Announcement (Feb 20, 1947)British PM Clement Attlee announced British would quit India by June 1948; appointed Mountbatten as last Viceroy to oversee transfer of power
Cabinet Mission Plan (May 1946)British plan for united India with federal structure (three tiers); Congress accepted; League initially rejected then accepted; Interim Government formed Sept 1946
Direct Action Day (Aug 16, 1946)Muslim League called; Great Calcutta Killings — communal riots; 5,000–10,000 killed; violence spread to Bihar, Noakhali; polarised Hindu-Muslim relations fatally
Mountbatten Plan (June 3, 1947)Partition of British India into India and Pakistan; Dominion status initially; provinces choose their dominion by vote; Punjab and Bengal partitioned; date moved up from June 1948 to August 1947
Indian Independence Act (July 18, 1947)Passed by British Parliament; two Dominions — India and Pakistan — effective from midnight August 14–15, 1947; princely states' paramountcy ended — free to join either or remain independent
Independence — August 15, 1947Nehru's "Tryst with Destiny" speech at midnight in Constituent Assembly; Lord Mountbatten — first GG of India; Pakistan created August 14; Gandhi stayed in Calcutta (not Delhi) working for communal peace
Radcliffe Award (Aug 17, 1947)Cyril Radcliffe (British lawyer, never visited India before) drew boundaries of India-Pakistan in 5 weeks; Punjab Boundary Commission + Bengal Boundary Commission; award announced 2 days after Independence — deliberate delay; Radcliffe never returned to India and reportedly burned all his papers; left deep grievances on both sides
Partition Violence & MigrationLargest mass migration in human history — approximately 1.5 crore (15 million) people crossed borders; estimated 2–5 lakh (200,000–500,000) killed in communal violence; Punjab worst affected; trains carrying refugees massacred; women abducted on both sides
January 26, 1950Constitution of India came into force; India became a Republic; Dr Rajendra Prasad — first President; Jan 26 chosen as Republic Day because Purna Swaraj was declared on this date in 1930
