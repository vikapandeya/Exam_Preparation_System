Kosi River — Sorrow of Bihar

FeatureDetail
Nickname"Sorrow of Bihar" — frequent devastating floods
OriginNepal Himalayas; joins Ganga at Kursela (Katihar district)
ProblemShifts course frequently (shifted ~100 km westward over centuries); called "wandering river"; carries enormous silt from Nepal; embanked 1955 by Indo-Nepal treaty
Flood areasSupaul, Madhepura, Saharsa, Darbhanga, Madhubani districts most affected
Kosi BarrageBhimnagar (Nepal border); controls flood; irrigation; bilateral project

Climate of Bihar

  • Summer (March–June): Very hot; 40–45°C; dry; Loo winds from Rajasthan
  • Monsoon (June–September): SW Monsoon; June onwards; 1000–1500mm rainfall in North Bihar; less in south; prone to floods
  • Autumn (October–November): Retreating monsoon; clear skies; festive season (Chhath, Diwali)
  • Winter (December–February): Cold; 4–10°C; dense fog in Gangetic plains; Rabi crop season; Western disturbances bring some rain

Chhath Puja — 4-Day Ritual in Detail

DayNameRitual
Day 1 — ChaturthiNahay KhayDevotees take a holy dip in Ganga/river; eat only lauki (bottle gourd) sabzi with rice; only one meal; purity and cleanliness begins
Day 2 — PanchamiKharna (Lohanda)Full-day fast without water; in the evening: Kheer (rice + jaggery), roti offered to Sun God; family shares it as prasad; 36-hour nirjala (waterless) fast begins after this
Day 3 — Shashthi (main day)Sandhya Arghya (Sunset offering)Devotees — mostly women — stand waist-deep in river/pond/tank; offer bamboo baskets (Soop) filled with seasonal fruits, Thekua, sugarcane, turmeric, ginger to the setting sun; Chhath folk songs sung by entire community
Day 4 — Saptami (dawn)Usha Arghya (Sunrise offering)Pre-dawn gathering at river; Arghya (offering) made to the rising sun as it emerges from water; 36-hour fast broken; prasad (Thekua, fruits) distributed to all present; festival concludes
📌 Why Chhath is unique: Only major Hindu festival where offerings are made to BOTH the setting sun AND the rising sun. No idol is worshipped — Nature (Surya, Ganga, sky) is revered directly. Dedicated to Surya Dev (Sun God) and Chhathi Maiya (Usha — goddess of dawn, sister of Sun). Inscribed on UNESCO Representative List of Intangible Cultural Heritage of Humanity. Celebrated in 140+ countries where Bihari diaspora lives.
