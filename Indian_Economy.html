📊 GDP & Economic Basics

Understanding GDP

ConceptDefinition
GDP (Gross Domestic Product)Total monetary value of all goods & services produced within a country in a year. Includes output by all residents (foreign + domestic) within boundaries.
GNP (Gross National Product)GDP + income earned by residents abroad – income earned by foreigners in India. Focuses on nationality, not location.
NNP at Market PriceGNP – Depreciation
NNP at Factor Cost (National Income)NNP at Market Price – Net Indirect Taxes
GDP at Constant Prices (Real GDP)GDP adjusted for inflation; measures actual growth; used to compare across years
GDP at Current Prices (Nominal GDP)GDP measured at current year prices; affected by both growth AND inflation
GDP DeflatorRatio of Nominal GDP to Real GDP × 100; measures price level changes
Base Year (India)2011-12 is the current base year for GDP calculation (changed from 2004-05)

Measuring GDP — Three Approaches

  • Output/Production Method: Sum of value added by all sectors (Agriculture + Industry + Services)
  • Income Method: Sum of all incomes earned (wages + rent + interest + profit)
    • Expenditure Method: C + I + G + (X–M) — Consumption + Investment + Government Spending + Net Exports

Key Monetary Policy Rates

RateDefinitionCurrent (approx)
Repo RateRate at which RBI lends to commercial banks (short-term, against securities)6.5%
Reverse Repo RateRate at which RBI borrows from commercial banks3.35% (Standing Deposit Facility Rate now used)
CRR (Cash Reserve Ratio)% of deposits banks must keep as cash with RBI; no interest earned4%
SLR (Statutory Liquidity Ratio)% of deposits banks must maintain in gold/approved securities18%
Bank RateRate at which RBI lends without collateral (long-term); now aligned with MSF rate6.75%
MSF (Marginal Standing Facility)Emergency overnight borrowing facility for banks; 25 bps above repo rate6.75%
SDF (Standing Deposit Facility)Replaces reverse repo; floor of interest rate corridor; no collateral needed from banks6.25%
📌 Monetary Policy Committee (MPC): 6-member committee (3 RBI + 3 Govt nominees); meets 6 times/year; decides Repo Rate by majority vote; Governor has casting vote. Target: CPI inflation 4% (±2%). Constituted 2016 under RBI Amendment Act.

Priority Sector Lending (PSL)

Banks must lend 40% of Adjusted Net Bank Credit (ANBC) to priority sectors. Includes: Agriculture (18%), Micro & Small Enterprises, Education, Housing, Social Infrastructure, Renewable Energy, Weaker Sections (10%), Export Credit.

📌 PSB Consolidation 2019-20: 10 public sector banks merged into 4 — Vijaya Bank + Dena Bank → Bank of Baroda; OBC + UBI → PNB; Andhra Bank + Corporation Bank → Union Bank; Syndicate Bank → Canara Bank. Total PSBs reduced from 27 to 12.
📑 Union Budget

Understanding the Union Budget

TermDefinition
Annual Financial Statement (Art. 112)Constitutional name for the Union Budget; statement of receipts and expenditures for a financial year
PresentationFinance Minister presents on February 1 (changed from last working day of Feb since 2017)
Railway BudgetMerged with Union Budget from 2017
Consolidated Fund of India (Art. 266)All revenues, loans, recovery of loans; all expenditure charged from here; Parliament's approval needed
Contingency Fund (Art. 267)Emergency spending without Parliament approval; held by President; ₹500 crore corpus; replenishment requires parliamentary approval
Public Account (Art. 266)Money received other than revenues (PF, small savings, deposits); executive approval sufficient

Revenue vs Capital Account

AccountReceiptsExpenditure
RevenueTax revenue (Direct + Indirect) + Non-tax revenue (fees, dividends, RBI surplus)Salaries, subsidies, interest payments, defence running costs, pensions — day-to-day expenses
CapitalBorrowings (domestic + external), disinvestment, recovery of loansCapital formation (roads, dams, buildings), loans to states, defence capital acquisition

Key Budget Deficits

DeficitFormulaSignificance
Revenue DeficitRevenue Expenditure – Revenue ReceiptsShows borrowing for consumption; bad if high
Fiscal DeficitTotal Expenditure – Total Receipts (excl. borrowings)= Government borrowing requirement; key indicator; FRBM target
Primary DeficitFiscal Deficit – Interest PaymentsShows deficit excluding past debt burden; measures current fiscal stance
Effective Revenue DeficitRevenue Deficit – Grants for Capital CreationMore meaningful indicator after grants to states
📌 FRBM Act (2003) — Fiscal Responsibility & Budget Management Act; targets fiscal consolidation. Original target: Fiscal Deficit ≤3% of GDP. Currently 5.1% target for FY2024-25; roadmap to 4.5% by FY2025-26.

🌾 Agriculture

Agricultural Overview

FeatureDetail
Contribution~18% GDP; ~42% employment; ~13% of total exports
Land UseNet Sown Area — ~140 million hectares; Total Cropped Area — ~198 million hectares
Kharif CropsSown in June-July; harvested Sept-Oct; Rain dependent. Rice, Maize, Jowar, Bajra, Cotton, Jute, Sugarcane, Groundnut

Green Revolution

AspectDetail
PeriodMid-1960s; triggered by food crisis (1965-66 droughts)
Father of Green Revolution (India)M.S. Swaminathan — developed High Yielding Variety (HYV) seeds
Father of Green Revolution (World)Norman Borlaug (Nobel Peace Prize 1970)
States benefitedPunjab, Haryana, Western UP (wheat); later Tamil Nadu, Karnataka (rice)
ResultFood grain production doubled; India achieved self-sufficiency by 1970s
CriticismIncreased use of chemicals, water depletion, soil degradation; regional disparity
