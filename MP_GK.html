🏛️ History of Madhya Pradesh

Prehistoric & Ancient Period

  • Bhimbetka Rock Shelters — Raisen district; 40,000-year-old paintings; UNESCO World Heritage Site; world's oldest known rock art; Stone Age evidence spanning Lower Paleolithic to Medieval period.
  • Adamgarh Hill (Hoshangabad/Narmadapuram) — Stone Age tools; one of the earliest sites of human habitation in India.
  • Avanti Mahajanapada — Ujjain was its capital; one of the 16 Mahajanapadas of Vedic period.
  • Mauryan Empire — Ashoka built Sanchi Stupa (3rd century BCE); UNESCO WHS; finest example of Buddhist architecture; located at Sanchi, Raisen district.
  • Gupta Empire (Golden Age) — Vidisha (Besnagar) was important centre; Heliodorus pillar.

Medieval Period

Dynasty / PeriodKey Fact
Paramara DynastyRaja Bhoja (1000–1055 AD) — great scholar-king; built Bhojtal (Upper Lake, Bhopal); Bhojpur Shiva temple; Bhopal named after him.
Chandela DynastyKhajuraho temples (10th–12th century) — 85 temples (25 surviving); UNESCO WHS; built in Bundelkhand.
Gondwana KingdomRani Durgavati (Gond queen, Jabalpur) — fought Akbar's general Asaf Khan in 1564; died in battle rather than surrender.
Malwa Sultanate / Mandu"Island of Joy" — Jahaz Mahal, Hindola Mahal; legendary Baz Bahadur–Rupmati love story.
Mughal PeriodMandu and Ujjain remained important centres.
Maratha PeriodHolkar dynasty (Indore) — Ahilya Bai Holkar; Scindia dynasty (Gwalior).

Modern & Independence Period

  • 1857 Revolt: Tatya Tope executed at Shivpuri. Rani Lakshmibai died fighting in Gwalior — buried at Phool Bagh, Gwalior.
  • Shankar Shah & Raghunath Shah — Gond chiefs, executed by British at Jabalpur in 1857; first tribal martyrs of the revolt.
  • Chandra Shekhar Azad — born at Bhavra village, Alirajpur; HSRA freedom fighter; died at Alfred Park, Allahabad (1931).
  • British era: MP was part of Central Provinces and Berar under British India.
  • 1 November 1956: Madhya Pradesh formed under States Reorganisation Act.
  • 1 November 2000: Chhattisgarh carved out from MP's eastern districts (MP's eastern 16 districts).
Exam Tip: Bhimbetka (prehistoric) → Sanchi (Mauryan/Buddhist) → Khajuraho (Chandela) → Mandu (Malwa Sultanate) → Rani Durgavati (Gond/Mughal) — learn the chronology.

🗺️ Geography of Madhya Pradesh

Location & Borders

MP is located in central India — truly the "Heart of India." It shares borders with 5 states: Uttar Pradesh (N), Chhattisgarh (E), Maharashtra (S), Gujarat (SW), Rajasthan (W). No international border. No sea coast (landlocked state).

Amarkantak (Anuppur district) — unique triple river origin: Narmada (west), Son (east), and Johila rivers originate from the same plateau. It is also an Achanakmar-Amarkantak Biosphere Reserve.

🎭 Culture & Festivals of MP

Classical Music — Tansen & Gwalior Gharana

  • Dhrupad — oldest form of Hindustani classical music; Gwalior Gharana is the most ancient and prestigious Dhrupad gharana.
  • Tansen (1493–1586) — greatest Indian classical musician; born at Behat near Gwalior; one of Akbar's Navaratnas; invented many ragas: Mian ki Todi, Mian ki Malhar, Raga Darbari Kanada.
  • Tansen Sangeet Samaroh — held at Gwalior every November/December; major classical music festival at Tansen's tomb.
  • Ustad Amjad Ali Khan — sarod maestro from Gwalior (Bangash family); Bharat Ratna awardee.

Major Festivals

  • Khajuraho Dance Festival — February; classical dances performed against the backdrop of UNESCO Khajuraho temples; 7-day national event.
  • Simhastha / Kumbh Mela — Ujjain; held every 12 years; one of the 4 Kumbh Mela sites; 2016 was last Simhastha.
  • Tansen Sangeet Samaroh — Gwalior; November–December; classical music.
  • Lokrang Utsav — Bhopal; January (Republic Day period); folk arts and tribal cultural fair.
  • Pachmarhi Utsav — November; tourism and cultural festival at MP's only hill station.

Cuisine, Handicrafts & GI Tags

  • Dal Bafla — baked dough balls (bafla) dipped in dal/ghee; MP's signature dish (similar to Rajasthan's dal baati).
  • Poha-Jalebi — Indore's famous breakfast combination; Indore is India's cleanest city.
  • Bhutte ka Kees — grated corn cooked with milk and spices; Indore specialty.
  • Chanderi Sarees (Ashoknagar) — GI tagged; UNESCO Intangible Cultural Heritage recognition; ultra-fine cotton & silk weave.
  • Maheshwari Sarees (Maheshwar, Khargone) — revived by Rani Ahilya Bai Holkar; GI tagged; on the Narmada riverbank.
  • Gond Painting — most famous tribal art form; international recognition — artist Bhajju Shyam illustrated "The London Jungle Book."
  • Pithora Art — Bhil tribal painting; vibrant ceremonial art from SW MP.
Remember: Chanderi (Ashoknagar) = cotton & silk weave. Maheshwari (Maheshwar) = revived by Ahilya Bai Holkar. Both are GI tagged MP textiles.

🐯 Wildlife & Tigers of MP

MP — "Tiger State of India"

Madhya Pradesh has the highest tiger population in India — ~785 tigers (2023 All India Tiger Estimation). MP has 6 Tiger Reserves. Project Tiger was launched in 1973 and Jim Corbett NP was the first, but MP leads in tiger count.

Major National Parks & Tiger Reserves

Project Cheetah — Kuno NP

  • Kuno National Park (Sheopur district) — chosen for cheetah reintroduction after careful habitat assessment.
  • 17 Sep 2022 — PM Modi released 8 cheetahs from Namibia at Kuno; India's most iconic wildlife reintroduction.
  • Feb 2023 — 12 more cheetahs brought from South Africa.
  • Cheetah had been declared extinct in India in 1952 (last seen in Koriya, Chhattisgarh/MP).

Other Protected Areas

  • Nauradehi Wildlife Sanctuary (Sagar) — largest wildlife sanctuary in MP; proposed tiger relocation site.
  • Achanakmar-Amarkantak Biosphere Reserve — spans MP–Chhattisgarh boundary; includes Amarkantak plateau.
  • Pachmarhi Biosphere Reserve — Satpura range; one of UNESCO's biosphere reserves; leopard, wild boar, wolf.
  • Ratapani Wildlife Sanctuary — Raisen; adjacent to Bhimbetka; being upgraded to tiger reserve.
White Tiger fact: First white tiger in the world was captured at Bandhavgarh forests (Rewa forests) in 1951 by Maharaja Martand Singh of Rewa. Mohan — the white tiger — became famous worldwide and all white tigers in captivity today descend from him.

🏕️ Tribes & Communities of MP

Overview

Madhya Pradesh has the highest Scheduled Tribe (ST) population among all Indian states — approximately 21% of state population. MP has 46 recognized tribal communities, including 3 Particularly Vulnerable Tribal Groups (PVTGs).

Major Tribes of MP

TribeRegion in MPKey Characteristics
GondMandla, Dindori, Balaghat, Chhindwara, SeoniLargest tribe in India (also in Telangana, Maharashtra, Chhattisgarh); skilled farmers; world-famous Gond paintings; Rani Durgavati was a Gond queen
BhilJhabua, Alirajpur, Dhar, Khargone (SW MP)2nd largest tribe in India; also in Rajasthan & Gujarat; Pithora tribal art; skilled archers; Tantya Bhil was a famous leader
BaigaMandla, Dindori, Balaghat (central MP)PVTG; practice bewar (slash & burn / shifting cultivation); sacred relationship with forests; traditional healers
KorkuBetul, Narmadapuram, Khandwa (Satpura region)Austro-Asiatic language group; forest dwellers of Satpura
SahariyaShivpuri, Guna, Ashoknagar (Gwalior-Chambal region)PVTG; most marginalized tribe in MP; forest-dependent; unique language
BhariaPatalkot valley, ChhindwaraPVTG; Patalkot is a hidden valley 1000 ft deep; also called Bhumia; traditional healers
HalbaBalaghat, SeoniAgricultural tribe; speak Halbi language

PVTGs & 5th Schedule Areas

  • Particularly Vulnerable Tribal Groups (PVTGs) in MP: Baiga, Sahariya, Bharia — 3 PVTGs.
  • 5th Schedule Areas (tribal self-governance): Jhabua, Alirajpur, Dhar (Bhil area); Mandla, Dindori (Gond/Baiga area).
  • PESA Act (1996) — Panchayats (Extension to Scheduled Areas) Act applies to MP's 5th Schedule areas; gram sabha has special powers.

Tribal Martyrs & Institutions

  • Shankar Shah & Raghunath Shah — Gond chiefs of Garha kingdom; executed by British at Jabalpur on 18 Sep 1857; first tribal martyrs of 1857 revolt in MP.
  • Tantya Bhil (1842–1889) — Bhil tribal leader from Khandwa; robbed British and zamindars to help poor — "Robin Hood of MP/India"; hanged by British 1889.
  • Indira Gandhi Rashtriya Janjati Vishwavidyalaya — tribal university at Amarkantak; dedicated to tribal education and culture.
  • Bhajju Shyam — Gond artist from Patangarh, Mandla; internationally acclaimed; illustrated "The London Jungle Book" for the British Museum.
Patalkot Valley — hidden valley in Tamia tehsil, Chhindwara; home to Bharia PVTG; ~79 sq km; accessible only through narrow gorges; one of the least-known tribal regions in India.

Bhopal Gas Tragedy (1984)

  • Date: Night of 2–3 December 1984.
  • Cause: Methyl Isocyanate (MIC) gas leak from Union Carbide India Ltd. (UCIL) pesticide plant at Bhopal.
  • Impact: 3,787+ official immediate deaths; actual estimates 15,000–25,000 deaths; thousands still suffer health effects.
  • Significance: Worst industrial disaster in human history; led to strengthening of India's environmental laws (EPA 1986).
  • Exam Note: MIC (Methyl Isocyanate) — used in manufacture of Sevin (carbaryl) pesticide; reacted with water in storage tank.

Tourism Economy

MP's major tourism circuits: Khajuraho (UNESCO temples) · Sanchi (Buddhist heritage) · Maheshwar & Mandu (historical) · Orchha (medieval Bundela capital) · Ujjain (religious — Mahakaleshwar Jyotirlinga) · Pachmarhi (hill station) · Kanha & Bandhavgarh (wildlife safaris). Indore — India's cleanest city 7 years in a row (Swachh Survekshan 2017–2023).

Administrative Structure

MP has 55 districts organised into 10 administrative divisions. The 3 newest districts are Mauganj (carved from Rewa), Pandhurna (from Chhindwara), and Maihar (from Satna).

Central Schemes with MP Relevance

  • One District One Product (ODOP) — MP has GI-tagged products assigned per district: Chanderi fabric (Ashoknagar), Maheshwari fabric (Khargone), Bagh prints (Dhar), etc.
  • PM Kisan Samman Nidhi — ₹6,000/year; MP has one of highest registered farmer beneficiaries. MP's Sambal Yojana adds another ₹6,000.
  • Project Tiger / Project Cheetah — MP is the most significant state for both conservation projects.
  • Bhopal Gas Relief — Ongoing state + central compensation and health care for Bhopal Gas Tragedy victims; BGPMUS (Bhopal Gas Peedit Mahila Udyog Sangathan) is key organisation.
Ladli Laxmi Yojana was launched in 2007 by MP; it was so successful that 6 other states replicated it and the Centre launched "Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana" inspired by this model. MP's Ladli Laxmi 2.0 extends benefits to higher education.
