🏛️ UP History

Ancient Period

  • Vedic Age: Much of the Rigveda and later Vedas composed in the Ganga-Yamuna doab region. Kuru and Panchala kingdoms flourished here.
  • Mahajanapadas (600–300 BCE): Kashi (Varanasi), Kosala (Ayodhya/Sravasti), Vatsa (Kaushambi/Prayagraj), Panchala (Ahichhatra)
  • Buddhism: Buddha gave his first sermon at Sarnath (Varanasi). Bodhi tree related pilgrimage circuit — Sarnath, Kushinagar (death/parinirvana) both in UP.
  • Mauryan Empire: Ashoka's famous Lion Capital pillar erected at Sarnath — now India's national emblem. Pataliputra (Bihar) was capital but UP was central to the empire.
  • Gupta Golden Age (4th–5th century CE): Prayagraj (Allahabad) was center of Gupta administration; Chandragupta II's era.

Medieval Period

  • Kannauj (7th century): Harsha Vardhana made Kannauj his capital; Hieun Tsang visited.
  • Rajput era: Gahadavalas of Varanasi, Chandelas of Bundelkhand (Khajuraho).
  • Delhi Sultanate: Ibrahim Lodi vs Babur — First Battle of Panipat (1526) decided Mughal rule.
  • Mughal Empire: Agra served as Mughal capital; Taj Mahal, Agra Fort, Fatehpur Sikri built. Akbar, Jahangir, Shah Jahan ruled from Agra/Lahore alternately.
  • Nawabs of Awadh (18th–19th century): Lucknow became cultural capital under Nawabs — Asaf-ud-Daula built Bara Imambara (1784).

State Formation

  • 1 April 1937: United Provinces of Agra & Oudh formed under Government of India Act 1935.
  • 24 January 1950: Renamed Uttar Pradesh (UP) at Republic of India's formation.
  • 9 November 2000: Uttarakhand (initially Uttaranchal) carved out as separate state from UP's Himalayan districts.

Physical Divisions

  • 1. Himalayan/Tarai Region (North): Narrow belt along Nepal border — dense forests, marshy land, rich wildlife (Dudhwa National Park). Districts: Pilibhit, Lakhimpur Kheri, Bahraich, Balrampur, Siddharthnagar, Maharajganj.
  • 2. Gangetic Plain (Centre): Vast fertile alluvial plain — the core of UP. Most populous zone. Covers ~70% of the state area.
  • 3. Bundelkhand/Vindhyan Plateau (South): Rocky, undulating terrain. Chambal ravines. Districts: Jhansi, Lalitpur, Mahoba, Banda, Chitrakoot, Hamirpur.

Soils & Doab Regions

Alluvial soil covers 80%+ of UP — most fertile type. Two sub-types:

  • Bhangar: Older alluvium, slightly elevated, kankar (limestone) nodules, less fertile.
  • Khadar: New alluvium, deposited by annual floods, highly fertile, low-lying.

Doab = land between two rivers. Key doabs in UP:

  • Ganga-Yamuna Doab (most famous — between Ganga and Yamuna)
  • Ganga-Ghaghra Doab (Awadh region)
  • Ghaghra-Gandak Doab (eastern UP)

Climate

Subtropical monsoon. Hot summers (April–June, 40–45°C in west), mild winters (Dec–Jan can drop to 3–5°C), monsoon (June–September). Western UP is hotter and drier; eastern UP gets more rainfall.

Key Fact: Dudhwa National Park (Lakhimpur Kheri) is UP's only National Park — home to tigers, one-horned rhinos (reintroduced), and swamp deer (barasingha).

🎭 Culture & Festivals

Lucknow — Nawabi Tehzeeb

  • Tehzeeb: Awadh's culture of refinement, politeness ("pehle aap" culture).
  • Chikankari: Delicate hand embroidery on white/pastel fabric — GI tagged, Lucknow.
  • Kathak Dance: One of India's 8 classical dances — Lucknow Gharana (Birju Maharaj); Jaipur Gharana also prominent.
  • Awadhi Cuisine: Dum Biryani, Galouti kebab, Kakori kebab, Shami kebab, Nihari.
  • Heritage Sites: Bara Imambara (1784, Asaf-ud-Daula), Chhota Imambara, Bhool Bhulaiya (labyrinth), British Residency.

Varanasi (Kashi) — Eternal City

  • One of the world's oldest continuously inhabited cities (~3,000+ years).
  • 88 Ghats along the Ganga — Dashashwamedh Ghat (famous Ganga Aarti), Manikarnika (cremation), Assi Ghat.
  • BHU: Banaras Hindu University — one of Asia's largest residential universities; founded by Madan Mohan Malaviya (1916).
  • Banarasi silk sarees — GI tagged; woven by Muslim weavers (bunkar) traditionally.
  • Sarnath (5 km away) — Buddha's first sermon; Dhamek Stupa, Ashoka Pillar.

Mathura-Vrindavan — Krishna Land

  • Mathura — birthplace of Lord Krishna; Janmashtami celebration.
  • Lathmar Holi (Barsana-Nandgaon) — women beat men with sticks; Phoolon ki Holi (Vrindavan Banke Bihari Temple) — celebrated with flowers.

Ayodhya — Ram Janmabhoomi

  • Birthplace of Lord Ram; Ram Mandir inaugurated January 2024.
  • Deepotsav: Diwali celebrated by lighting lakhs of diyas on Saryu ghats — world record events.
  • Ram Navami, Ramnavami mela — major pilgrimages.

Prayagraj — Sangam City

  • Sangam: Confluence of Ganga, Yamuna, and mythical Saraswati.
  • Kumbh Mela (every 12 years) — UNESCO Intangible Cultural Heritage; world's largest human gathering. Maha Kumbh 2025 attracted record pilgrims. Single day record: 4.5 crore+ (2019 Ardh Kumbh).
  • Ardh Kumbh every 6 years; Magh Mela annually.

Famous Food Specialties

  • Petha (Agra): Ash gourd candy — GI tagged; Agra ka Petha is iconic.
  • Makkhan Malai/Malaiyo (Varanasi): Frothy winter sweet made from churned milk — only in winter months.
  • Bedai-Jalebi: Popular breakfast in Varanasi and eastern UP.
  • Lucknowi Chaat: Matar ki chaat, Basket chaat — uniquely rich and creamy.
  • Dum Biryani (Lucknow/Awadhi): Slow-cooked (dum pukht) biryani with aromatic spices.
GI Tagged Products of UP (Key): Banarasi Silk, Lucknow Chikankari, Lucknow Zardozi, Agra Petha, Mathura Peda, Bhadohi Carpets, Moradabad Brass, Firozabad Glass, Allahabad Guava, Dussehri Mango.

Administrative Divisions

UP has 18 Divisions and 75 Districts. Each division is headed by a Divisional Commissioner. Each district is headed by a District Magistrate (DM).

Below DM: SDM (Sub-Divisional Magistrate) → Tehsildar → Naib Tehsildar (Revenue administration).

Urban Local Bodies

UP has 17 Nagar Nigams (Municipal Corporations). Major ones: Lucknow, Kanpur, Agra, Varanasi, Prayagraj, Ghaziabad, Meerut, Aligarh, Bareilly, Gorakhpur.

Special Forces & Bodies

  • PAC (Provincial Armed Constabulary): UP's state paramilitary force; over 80,000 personnel; used for riot control and internal security.
  • UP STF (Special Task Force): Elite anti-crime unit.
  • UP ATS (Anti-Terrorist Squad): Counter-terrorism operations.
  • Revenue Courts: Administered by Lekhpal → Kanungo → SDM → DM → Board of Revenue.
Unique feature: UP has a bicameral legislature — both Vidhan Sabha and Vidhan Parishad — unlike many states that have only Vidhan Sabha. States with bicameral legislature in India: UP, Bihar, Maharashtra, Karnataka, Andhra Pradesh, Telangana (6 states).
