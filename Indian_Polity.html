📚 Quick Reference — Must-Know Facts
KEY FACT
Constituent Assembly
Formed July 1946; Dr B.R. Ambedkar — Chairman of Drafting Committee; Dr Rajendra Prasad — President of Assembly. Constitution adopted 26 Nov 1949, enforced 26 Jan 1950.
KEY FACT
Borrowed Features
Parliamentary system (UK), Fundamental Rights (USA), DPSP (Ireland), Federal structure (Canada), Emergency (Germany), Amendment procedure (South Africa), Concurrent List (Australia).
KEY FACT
Part III — Fundamental Rights
Articles 12–35. Six FRs: Equality (14–18), Freedom (19–22), Against Exploitation (23–24), Religion (25–28), Culture & Education (29–30), Constitutional Remedies (32).
KEY FACT
Article 32 vs 226
Art. 32 — Right to move Supreme Court for FR enforcement (itself a FR). Art. 226 — High Court can issue writs for both FR and other legal rights. Broader jurisdiction for HC.
KEY FACT
Five Writs
Habeas Corpus (produce body), Mandamus (command to perform duty), Prohibition (stop inferior court from exceeding jurisdiction), Certiorari (quash order), Quo Warranto (by what authority).
KEY FACT
Schedules Overview
1st: States & UTs. 2nd: Salaries. 3rd: Oaths. 4th: RS seats. 5th: SC/ST areas. 6th: Tribal areas (NE). 7th: Union/State/Concurrent lists. 8th: 22 languages. 9th: Land reform acts. 10th: Anti-defection. 11th: Panchayat subjects. 12th: Municipality subjects.

📜 Preamble to the Constitution

The Preamble (as amended by 42nd Amendment, 1976)

"WE, THE PEOPLE OF INDIA, having solemnly resolved to constitute India into a SOVEREIGN SOCIALIST SECULAR DEMOCRATIC REPUBLIC and to secure to all its citizens:

JUSTICE, social, economic and political;
LIBERTY of thought, expression, belief, faith and worship;
EQUALITY of status and of opportunity;
and to promote among them all
FRATERNITY assuring the dignity of the individual and the unity and integrity of the Nation;

IN OUR CONSTITUENT ASSEMBLY this twenty-sixth day of November, 1949, do HEREBY ADOPT, ENACT AND GIVE TO OURSELVES THIS CONSTITUTION."

Key Words Explained

WordMeaningAdded When
SovereignIndia is independent — not subject to any foreign powerOriginal 1950
SocialistMixed economy; state ownership with private enterprise; equitable distribution42nd Amendment 1976
SecularNo state religion; all religions treated equally42nd Amendment 1976
DemocraticGovernment by representatives elected by people; universal adult suffrageOriginal 1950
RepublicElected head of state (President) — not hereditaryOriginal 1950
⚖️ Berubari Case (1960): SC held Preamble is not part of the Constitution. Kesavananda Bharati Case (1973): SC held Preamble IS part of Constitution and can be amended but Basic Structure cannot be destroyed.
🛡️ Fundamental Rights (Part III — Articles 12–35)

Six Fundamental Rights

RightArticlesKey Provisions
Right to Equality14–18Art 14: Equality before law | Art 15: No discrimination on race/religion/sex/caste/place of birth | Art 16: Equal opportunity in public employment | Art 17: Abolition of Untouchability | Art 18: Abolition of Titles (except military/academic)
Right to Freedom19–22Art 19: Six freedoms (speech, assembly, association, movement, residence, profession) | Art 20: Protection in respect of conviction | Art 21: Right to Life & Personal Liberty | Art 21A: Right to Education | Art 22: Protection against arbitrary arrest
Right against Exploitation23–24Art 23: Prohibition of traffic in human beings & forced labour | Art 24: Prohibition of employment of children under 14 in hazardous occupations
Right to Freedom of Religion25–28Art 25: Freedom of conscience, profession, practice, propagation | Art 26: Manage religious affairs | Art 27: No compulsion to pay taxes for promotion of religion | Art 28: No religious instruction in state-funded institutions
Cultural & Educational Rights29–30Art 29: Protection of interests of minorities | Art 30: Right of minorities to establish & administer educational institutions
Right to Constitutional Remedies32–35Art 32: Right to move SC — "Heart & Soul" of Constitution (Ambedkar) | Five Writs: Habeas Corpus, Mandamus, Prohibition, Certiorari, Quo Warranto
📌 Originally 7 FRs — Right to Property was a FR under Art 31 but removed by 44th Amendment (1978) and made a legal right under Art 300A.

🚨 Emergency Provisions

Three Types of Emergency

TypeArticleGroundProclaimed byApprovalEffect
National Emergency352External aggression or Armed rebellion (changed from "internal disturbance" by 44th Amendment)President (on cabinet advice)Special majority (2/3 present & voting + majority of total membership) within 1 month; each HouseFR under Art 19 suspended; Centre can give directions to states; Parliament can legislate on State List
President's Rule (State Emergency)356Failure of constitutional machinery in statePresident (on Governor's report or otherwise)Simple majority of Parliament within 2 months; then every 6 months (max 3 years)President assumes functions of State Govt; Parliament legislates for state
Financial Emergency360Financial stability or credit of India or any part threatenedPresidentSimple majority of Parliament within 2 months; no max periodPresident can give financial directions to states; reduction of salaries of all govt servants
📌 Proclamations of National Emergency: 1962 (China War), 1971 (Pakistan War), 1975 (Internal Emergency — most controversial). All three were revoked by 1977.

🗺️ Centre-State Relations & Federalism

7th Schedule — Three Legislative Lists

ListSubjectsWho legislates
Union List (List I)98 subjects — Defence, Foreign affairs, Currency, Railways, Banking, Atomic energyOnly Parliament
State List (List II)59 subjects — Public order, Police, Public health, Agriculture, Local Govt, LandOnly State Legislature (normally)
Concurrent List (List III)52 subjects — Education, Marriage, Forests, Trade unions, Criminal law, EvidenceBoth (Parliament prevails in conflict)
📌 Residuary powers (unlisted subjects) vest with Parliament (Art. 248) — unlike USA where residuary powers vest with states.

When Parliament can legislate on State List

  • Art. 249: Rajya Sabha passes resolution by 2/3 majority (national interest)
  • Art. 250: During National Emergency
  • Art. 252: Two or more states request Parliament to legislate
  • Art. 253: Implementation of international treaties/agreements
  • Art. 356: During President's Rule in state
