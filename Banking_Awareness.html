🏛️ RBI & Monetary Policy

Reserve Bank of India — Profile

ParameterDetail
Established1 April 1935 (recommended by Hilton Young Commission, 1926)
Nationalised1 January 1949
HeadquartersMumbai, Maharashtra
Governor (Current)Sanjay Malhotra (25th Governor, took charge Dec 2024)
Deputy Governors4 Deputy Governors (including one from commercial banking)
RBI ActReserve Bank of India Act, 1934
Motto"सर्वे भवन्तु सुखिनः" (May all be Happy)

Functions of RBI

  • Issue of Currency Notes: Sole authority to issue currency notes (except ₹1 coin and ₹1 note — issued by Ministry of Finance/Govt of India)
  • Banker's Bank: Holds CRR deposits of banks, acts as clearing house
  • Banker to Govt: Manages govt's accounts, issues Govt securities
  • Lender of Last Resort: Provides emergency liquidity to banks facing a crisis
  • Monetary Policy: Controls money supply and inflation through policy rates
  • Foreign Exchange Management: Manages FOREX reserves; implements FEMA 1999
  • Regulation & Supervision: Licenses, regulates and supervises banks, NBFCs
  • Credit Control: Quantitative (CRR, SLR, Repo) and Qualitative (margin requirements) tools
  • Developmental Role: Promotes financial inclusion, priority sector lending norms

Monetary Policy Committee (MPC)

  • Constituted under Section 45ZB of RBI Act (amended 2016)
  • 6 Members: 3 from RBI (Governor + 2 Deputy Governors/officials) + 3 external members appointed by Govt of India
  • Meets every 2 months (bi-monthly); decisions by majority vote; Governor has casting vote
  • Objective: Maintain inflation at 4% ± 2% (i.e., 2%–6%) based on CPI

Key Policy Rates (Current)

RateValueDescription
Repo Rate6.50%Short-term borrowing rate of banks from RBI against G-Secs (repurchase agreements)
Reverse Repo Rate3.35%Rate at which RBI absorbs liquidity by borrowing from banks
Marginal Standing Facility (MSF)6.75%Banks can borrow overnight funds from RBI above their SLR limit; 1% above Repo
Bank Rate6.75%Rate for long-term loans; penal rate for CRR/SLR shortfall
Cash Reserve Ratio (CRR)4.00%% of Net Demand and Time Liabilities (NDTL) kept as cash with RBI; earns no interest
Statutory Liquidity Ratio (SLR)18.00%% of NDTL maintained in approved liquid assets (gold, G-Secs, SDL)
Standing Deposit Facility (SDF)6.25%Introduced April 2022; floor of LAF corridor; banks park excess funds without collateral

Key RBI Tools & Mechanisms

  • Open Market Operations (OMO): RBI buys/sells G-Secs to inject/absorb liquidity in the system
  • Liquidity Adjustment Facility (LAF): Daily repo & reverse repo operations to manage short-term liquidity; corridor between SDF (floor) and MSF (ceiling)
  • Standing Deposit Facility (SDF): Introduced April 2022 as new floor of LAF corridor (replaced fixed reverse repo); no collateral required
  • Market Stabilisation Scheme (MSS): Sterilisation of excess foreign exchange inflows through issuance of G-Secs/T-Bills
  • MCLR (Marginal Cost of Funds based Lending Rate): Introduced April 2016; benchmark for bank loan pricing; replaced Base Rate system
Inflation Targeting: RBI is mandated to keep CPI inflation at 4% ± 2% band. If inflation stays outside band for 3 consecutive quarters, RBI must explain to Govt in writing.

Government Securities

  • G-Secs (Government Securities): Bonds issued by Central Govt; tenor 1–40 years; sovereign guarantee; traded on NDS-OM platform
  • T-Bills: Short-term zero-coupon G-Secs (91/182/364 days)
  • SDL (State Development Loans): Bonds issued by State Governments; slightly higher yield than G-Secs
  • STRIPS: Separate Trading of Registered Interest and Principal of Securities — zero-coupon bonds
SEBI's Triple Role: Regulator (of markets & intermediaries), Developmental (promotes investor awareness), Protective (protects investor interests from unfair practices like insider trading).

📊 Budget & Economy

Union Budget — Key Concepts

  • Presented by the Finance Minister on 1st February (changed from last working day of Feb in 2017)
  • Railway Budget merged with Union Budget from 2017
  • Constitutional provision: Article 112 (Annual Financial Statement)
  • Appropriation Bill must be passed for withdrawal from Consolidated Fund of India

Budget Accounts

AccountItems
Revenue Account (Receipts)Tax revenue (Direct + Indirect) + Non-tax revenue (dividends, interest, fees)
Revenue Account (Expenditure)Salaries, pensions, interest payments, subsidies, maintenance
Capital Account (Receipts)Borrowings (market loans, external), disinvestment, small savings
Capital Account (Expenditure)Capital formation, loans to states, repayment of borrowings

Deficit Types — Explained

DeficitFormulaSignificance
Revenue DeficitRevenue Expenditure − Revenue ReceiptsIndicates government is borrowing for current consumption (bad sign)
Fiscal DeficitTotal Expenditure − Total Receipts (excluding borrowings)Total borrowing requirement; printed in budget; most discussed deficit
Primary DeficitFiscal Deficit − Interest PaymentsFiscal deficit exclusive of past debt burden; measure of current fiscal management
Effective Revenue DeficitRevenue Deficit − Grants for Capital FormationIntroduced in India's budget from 2011-12

FRBM Act 2003

  • Fiscal Responsibility and Budget Management Act, 2003
  • Aims to eliminate Revenue Deficit and bring Fiscal Deficit to 3% of GDP
  • Escape clause allows 0.5% deviation in exceptional circumstances

Finance Commission (Art. 280)

  • Quasi-judicial body; constituted every 5 years
  • Recommends vertical devolution (Centre to States) and horizontal distribution (among states)
  • 15th Finance Commission (2020-25): Chaired by N.K. Singh; recommended 41% share for states (was 42% in 14th FC, 1% for J&K)
  • 16th Finance Commission: Constituted 2023, chaired by Arvind Panagariya; covers 2026-31
NITI Aayog replaced Planning Commission on 1 January 2015. Chaired by PM; no power to allocate funds; acts as think tank. No Five Year Plans — instead 15-year Vision, 7-year Strategy, 3-year Action Plan.

📉 Economic Indicators

National Income Concepts

ConceptFormula / Detail
GDP (Gross Domestic Product)Total value of goods & services produced within country's borders in a year; at market prices
GNP (Gross National Product)GDP + Net Factor Income from Abroad (NFIA)
NNP (Net National Product)GNP − Depreciation (Capital Consumption Allowance)
NNP at Factor CostNNP at Market Price − Net Indirect Taxes = National Income
GVA (Gross Value Added)GVA + Taxes on Products − Subsidies on Products = GDP
Nominal GDPGDP at current prices (not adjusted for inflation)
Real GDPGDP adjusted for inflation (at constant prices) — better measure of actual growth

Inflation Indices

IndexDetail
CPI (Consumer Price Index)Measures retail inflation; RBI's primary inflation target: 4% ±2%; released by MoSPI; base year 2012
WPI (Wholesale Price Index)Measures wholesale-level inflation; managed by DPIIT; base year 2011-12; covers 697 commodities
Core InflationCPI excluding food and fuel — reflects demand-side inflation; more persistent
IIP (Index of Industrial Production)Measures industrial output; base year 2011-12; covers Mining, Manufacturing, Electricity; released monthly by MoSPI

Balance of Payments (BoP)

  • Current Account: Trade in goods (visible) + Trade in services (invisible) + Transfer payments (remittances). CAD = Current Account Deficit
  • Capital Account: FDI, FII/FPI, External Borrowings, ECBs, NRI deposits
  • Financial Account: Reserve assets, monetary gold, SDRs
  • BOP Identity: Current Account + Capital Account + Financial Account = 0 (always balances)

IMF & SDR

  • SDR (Special Drawing Right): International reserve asset created by IMF; value based on basket of 5 currencies — USD, Euro, GBP, Yen, RMB (Chinese Yuan added 2016)
  • IMF provides BoP support to member countries through lending facilities
  • India is one of top 10 quota holders in IMF

💳 Digital Banking & UPI

  • Set up in 2008 under RBI guidance; an umbrella organisation for all retail payment systems in India
  • Not-for-profit entity owned by consortium of banks; HQ Mumbai
  • Operates: UPI, RuPay, IMPS, NACH, BBPS, NETC (FASTag), AEPS, BHIM, CTS

Other Digital Payment Instruments

  • NACH (National Automated Clearing House): Bulk recurring payments — salary credits, EMI debits, dividend, pension, subsidy DBT
  • BBPS (Bharat Bill Payment System): Interoperable platform for bill payments across electricity, water, gas, telecom, insurance
  • FASTag: RFID-based electronic toll collection; mandatory for all vehicles since Feb 2021; linked to prepaid wallet/bank
  • RuPay Card: Domestic payment network by NPCI; alternative to Visa/Mastercard; zero/low transaction cost; used in Jan Dhan accounts
  • AEPS (Aadhaar Enabled Payment System): Allows banking transactions using Aadhaar authentication at business correspondents
  • CBDC / e-Rupee: Central Bank Digital Currency launched by RBI Dec 2022; e₹-W (wholesale pilot) and e₹-R (retail pilot); legal tender
  • Account Aggregator (AA): RBI-regulated framework allowing sharing of financial data across institutions with user consent
  • Payment Aggregators: Entities facilitating online payment collections (Razorpay, PayU, CCAvenue) — regulated by RBI since 2021
Digital India targets: India is 2nd largest digital payments market globally. UPI crossed 10 billion transactions/month in 2023. RBI's vision: less-cash society through increased digital penetration.
