Key Battles & Events

Battle / EventYearSignificance
Battle of Tarain (1st)1191Prithviraj Chauhan III defeated Muhammad of Ghor
Battle of Tarain (2nd)1192Muhammad of Ghor defeated Prithviraj — end of Hindu rule in Delhi
Battle of Khanwa1527Babur defeated Rana Sanga (Mewar) — consolidated Mughal power

Maharana Pratap (1540–1597): Ruler of Mewar, refused to submit to Akbar. His horse Chetak is legendary. Battle of Haldighati — June 18, 1576. Never fully defeated; regained most of Mewar except Chittorgarh.

Rajput-Mughal Relations

  • Raja Man Singh I (Amber): Served as Akbar's most trusted general; married into Mughal family
  • Akbar: Built Fatehpur Sikri near Agra; had matrimonial alliances with Rajput chiefs; Jodha Bai (Harkha Bai) from Amber
  • Akbar-Pratap conflict: Most Rajput chiefs accepted Mughal suzerainty; Maharana Pratap of Mewar was the foremost exception
  • Aurangzeb: Ended policy of religious tolerance; led to Rajput alienation

Integration of Rajasthan (1948–1949)

  • Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel integrated 19 princely states in 7 stages
  • Matsya Union (February 1948) — first step (Alwar, Bharatpur, Dhaulpur, Karauli)
  • Rajasthan Union (March 1948) — southern Rajputana states
  • Greater Rajasthan (March 1949) — Jaipur merged; capital set at Jaipur
  • Final merger: Ajmer-Merwara and Sirohi integrated by 1956
  • Rajasthan Day: 30 March 1949
Prithviraj Chauhan III — known as "Rai Pithora," last great Hindu emperor of Delhi Sultanate era. Composed Prithviraj Raso (by Chand Bardai). Capital: Ajmer and Delhi.

Physical Features

Thar Desert

  • India's largest hot desert; part of the Great Indian Desert
  • Covers about 61% of Rajasthan's area; mainly in western Rajasthan
  • Annual rainfall < 250 mm; sparse vegetation (khardong, cactus, thorny shrubs)
  • Camel — "Ship of the Desert" — primary mode of transport
  • Sam Sand Dunes (Jaisalmer), Khuri Dunes are famous tourist spots

Aravalli Range

  • Oldest fold mountains in India (Pre-Cambrian, ~2,500 million years old)
  • Runs NE–SW direction, from Gujarat to Delhi (~692 km length)
  • Divides Rajasthan into North-Western desert region and South-Eastern fertile plain
  • Highest peak: Guru Shikhar (1,722 m) at Mt. Abu — only hill station of Rajasthan
  • Other peaks: Ser (1,597 m), Delwara (1,442 m)

Folk Music

  • Manganiars: Muslim hereditary musicians; instruments — sarangi, khartal, dholak; primarily from Barmer-Jaisalmer belt; UNESCO-recognized
  • Langas: Muslim community from Barmer; sindhi sarangi, morchang; perform for Sindhi Sipahi (Rajput) patrons
  • Bhopa-Bhopi: Performers of Pabuji ki Phad — a painted scroll; Bhopa sings while Bhopi holds the Phad lamp
  • Kathputli: String puppet theatre — Rajasthan's most iconic performing art; puppeteers (Bhat community) of Jaipur

📍 Districts of Rajasthan

District Structure

Rajasthan originally had 33 districts. In August 2023, 17 new districts were created, bringing the total to 50 districts. Additionally, 3 new divisions were added, making 10 divisions total.

New 17 Districts (2023): Anupgarh, Balotara, Beawar, Deeg, Didwana-Kuchaman, Dudu, Gangapurcity, Jaipur Rural, Jodhpur Rural, Kekdi, Khairthal-Tijara, Kotputli-Behror, Neem Ka Thana, Phalodi, Salumbar, Sanchore, Shahpura.
