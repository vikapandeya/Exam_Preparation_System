🌿 Ecology Basics

Ecosystem Components

Biotic: Producers (autotrophs), Consumers (herbivore/carnivore/omnivore), Decomposers (fungi, bacteria)

Abiotic: Temperature, water, sunlight, soil, wind, minerals

Food Chain & Food Web

Food chain = linear transfer of energy. Food web = interconnected food chains (more stable).

Trophic Levels: Producers (T1) → Primary consumers (T2) → Secondary (T3) → Tertiary (T4)

10% Law (Lindemann): Only 10% energy transferred to next trophic level.

Ecological Pyramids

PyramidAlways Upright?Exception
NumbersNoInverted in tree ecosystem (parasites)
BiomassNoInverted in aquatic (phytoplankton)
EnergyAlways uprightNo exception

Biogeochemical Cycles

  • Carbon cycle: Photosynthesis (sink), Respiration/Combustion (source), Oceans as carbon sink
  • Nitrogen cycle: N₂ fixation → Nitrification → Assimilation → Ammonification → Denitrification
  • Water cycle: Evaporation → Condensation → Precipitation → Run-off
  • Phosphorus cycle: Sedimentary cycle — no atmospheric phase

Ecological Succession

Primary: Bare rock/new area (pioneer = lichens). Secondary: Disturbed area with soil. Culminates in climax community (stable).

🦋 Biodiversity

Types of Biodiversity

  • Genetic diversity: Variation within a species (e.g., rice varieties)
  • Species diversity: Variety of species in a region
  • Ecosystem diversity: Variety of habitats and ecosystems

India's Biodiversity

India = megadiverse country (one of 17). Covers 2.4% land area but has 7–8% of world's species.

  • ~45,000 plant species, ~91,000 animal species recorded
  • Endemism: ~33% plants, many reptiles and amphibians

🌡️ Climate Change

Greenhouse Effect

Solar radiation passes through atmosphere → Earth absorbs and re-emits infrared → GHGs trap heat → Natural warming (necessary). Enhanced GHE = Global Warming.

IPCC (Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change)

Founded 1988 by WMO + UNEP. HQ: Geneva. Publishes Assessment Reports every 6–7 years. AR6 (2021–22): warming is "unequivocal" human influence.

Paris Agreement (2015)

  • Limit global warming to well below 2°C, preferably 1.5°C above pre-industrial levels
  • Countries submit NDCs (Nationally Determined Contributions)
  • India's NDC: 45% reduction in emission intensity by 2030, 50% electricity from non-fossil by 2030, net zero by 2070

El Niño & La Niña

El Niño: Warming of central/eastern Pacific → drought in Australia, India; floods in Peru/Ecuador. Weakens Indian monsoon.

La Niña: Cooling of Pacific → stronger Indian monsoon, floods in Australia.

India declared State of Climate Emergency — Mission LiFE (Lifestyle for Environment) launched by PM Modi at COP26 (Glasgow 2021).

💨 Pollution

201–300PoorBreathing discomfort for most people
301–400Very PoorRespiratory illness on prolonged exposure
401–500SevereHealthy people affected; serious impact on vulnerable

Water Pollution

  • BOD (Biochemical Oxygen Demand): O₂ needed to decompose organic matter. Higher BOD = more polluted.
  • Eutrophication: Excess nutrients (N, P) → algal bloom → O₂ depletion → dead zone
  • Biomagnification: Toxin concentration increases up food chain (DDT, mercury)

Soil Pollution

Causes: Pesticides (DDT, BHC), heavy metals (Cd, Pb, Hg), industrial waste, plastic. Remediation: bioremediation (microbes), phytoremediation (plants like Vetiver grass).

Ramsar Wetland Sites in India

India has 75+ Ramsar sites (as of 2023) — most in any country. Key ones:

  • Chilika Lake (Odisha) — largest coastal lagoon in India; first Ramsar site (1981)
  • Keoladeo Ghana (Bharatpur) — migratory bird paradise
  • Wular Lake (J&K) — largest freshwater lake in India
  • Loktak Lake (Manipur) — floating islands (phumdis)
  • Harike (Punjab) — India's largest constructed wetland
Protected Area Network: National Parks (strict — human activities prohibited), Wildlife Sanctuaries (some activities allowed), Conservation Reserves, Community Reserves, Biosphere Reserves (core + buffer + transition zones).
