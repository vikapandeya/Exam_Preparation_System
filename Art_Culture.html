🏛️ Architecture

Evolution of Indian Architecture

Indus Valley Civilisation (3300–1300 BC)

  • Grid-pattern town planning; baked brick structures; drainage systems
  • Great Bath — Mohenjo-daro; earliest public water tank
  • Granary — Harappa and Mohenjo-daro
  • Dholavira — UNESCO WHS (2021); sophisticated water harvesting; stadium-like space

Mauryan Period (322–185 BC)

  • Rock-cut caves (Barabar caves — first in India); polished sandstone
  • Ashokan Pillars — monolithic; polished sandstone; lion capital of Sarnath = National Emblem of India; Brahmi script edicts
  • Sanchi Stupa — Buddhist monument; UNESCO WHS; great torana (gateway) carvings; no image of Buddha in early phase (symbols used)

Post-Mauryan Schools of Art (2nd BC–3rd AD)

SchoolRegionKey Feature
GandharaNW India (modern Pakistan/Afghanistan)Greco-Buddhist; first images of Buddha in human form; Hellenistic features
MathuraUttar PradeshIndigenous; red sandstone; earthy; Buddha + Jain images
AmaravatiAndhra PradeshWhite limestone; decorative medallions; narrative friezes; movement & grace

Gupta Period (4th–6th AD) — "Golden Age"

  • Temple architecture begins; Nagara (north) style emerges
  • Deogarh Temple — Dasavatara temple; early Nagara style
  • Ajanta paintings — peak artistic achievement
  • Nalanda University — Buddhist learning centre

North Indian Temple Architecture (Nagara Style)

  • Shikhara (curvilinear tower) is the defining feature
  • Three sub-styles: Rekha-Prasad (main shikhara), Pida (porch), Khakara (roof)
  • Konark Sun Temple — Odisha; chariot wheel design; UNESCO WHS (1984)
  • Khajuraho Temples — Madhya Pradesh; erotic sculptures; Chandela dynasty; UNESCO WHS (1986)

  • Thirukkural — Thiruvalluvar; 1,330 couplets; ethics, polity, love; "Tamil Veda"
  • Silappatikaram & Manimegalai — major Sangam epics

Jainism

24 Tirthankaras (Ford-makers); last and most important: Mahavira (Vardhamana, 599–527 BC)

  • Born: Kundagrama (Vaishali district, Bihar); attained Nirvana at Pavapuri, Bihar
  • 5 Vows (Pancha Mahavrata): Ahimsa (non-violence), Satya (truth), Asteya (non-stealing), Brahmacharya (celibacy), Aparigraha (non-possession)
  • 3 Ratnas (Triratna): Right faith, Right knowledge, Right conduct
  • Digambara (sky-clad — no clothes) vs Shvetambara (white-clad)
  • First Tirthankara: Rishabhadeva (Adinatha)

Sikhism

  • Guru Nanak Dev (1469–1539) — founder; born Nankana Sahib (now Pakistan)
  • 10 Gurus: Nanak → Angad → Amar Das → Ram Das → Arjan Dev → Hargobind → Har Rai → Har Krishan → Tegh Bahadur → Gobind Singh (10th & last human Guru)
  • Guru Granth Sahib — eternal Guru; compiled by Guru Arjan Dev (5th Guru, 1604); final form by Guru Gobind Singh
  • Khalsa Panth — founded 1699 (Baisakhi) by Guru Gobind Singh at Anandpur Sahib; 5 Ks (Panj Kakars)
  • Golden Temple (Harmandir Sahib) — Amritsar; built by Guru Arjan Dev; open on 4 sides (all are welcome)

Islam in India

  • Arab traders — Islam reaches Kerala coast (7th c); Cheraman Juma Mosque (629 AD) — first mosque in India
  • Delhi Sultanate — Qutb-ud-din Aibak (1206); political establishment of Islam in North India
  • Mughal Empire (1526–1857) — peak of Islamic architecture and culture in India
  • Sufi Saints: Khwaja Moinuddin Chishti (Ajmer — Dargah Sharif); Nizamuddin Auliya (Delhi); Qutbuddin Bakhtiyar Kaki; Fariduddin Ganj-i-Shakar (Baba Farid)
  • Chishti order — most popular Sufi order in India; emphasised love, music (qawwali), and social equality

Christianity in India

  • St. Thomas (52 AD) — tradition holds he arrived in Kerala; established one of world's earliest Christian communities
  • Portuguese missionaries (16th c) — Francis Xavier in Goa (Basilica of Bom Jesus — his relics preserved; UNESCO WHS)
  • Syrian Christians — ancient Christians of Kerala (Thomas Christians / Nasranis)
  • Se Cathedral, Goa — largest church in Asia; built by Portuguese in 1619
