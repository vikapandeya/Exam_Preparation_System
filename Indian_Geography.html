🏔️ Physical Features of India

Major Physiographic Divisions

DivisionAreaKey Features
The Himalayas~5 lakh km²Three parallel ranges: Greater Himalayas (Himadri), Lesser Himalayas (Himachal), Outer Himalayas (Shiwaliks). World's youngest fold mountains. Origin: Tethys Sea sediments folded by Indian plate collision with Eurasian plate.
Northern Plains~7 lakh km²Formed by alluvial deposits of Indus-Ganga-Brahmaputra. Most fertile land. Bhangar (old alluvium), Khadar (new alluvium). Stretches 2400 km, 240–320 km wide.
Peninsular Plateau~16 lakh km²Ancient crystalline igneous and metamorphic rocks. Two parts: Central Highlands (north of Narmada) and Deccan Plateau (south). Chota Nagpur Plateau (mineral rich).
Indian Desert (Thar)~3.2 lakh km²In Rajasthan west of Aravalli. Low rainfall (<150mm/yr). Sandy terrain; Luni river drains into Rann of Kutch. Jaisalmer — driest area.
Coastal PlainsNarrow stripsWestern Ghats coast (Konkan, Karnataka, Malabar coasts — narrow, steep). Eastern Coromandel & Northern Circars coasts — wider, deltaic. Bay of Bengal coasts more fertile.
Islands1,382 islandsAndaman & Nicobar (Bay of Bengal — 572 islands; volcanic origin). Lakshadweep (Arabian Sea — 36 islands; coral origin).
📌 Western Ghats vs Eastern Ghats: Western Ghats — continuous, higher (avg 1000m), biodiversity hotspot. Eastern Ghats — discontinuous, lower (avg 600m), cut by rivers. Highest peak: Anamudi (Western Ghats, Kerala, 2695m).

Types of Rainfall in India

  • Convectional Rainfall: Hot regions — due to thermal heating; afternoon thundershowers (Assam, Bengal in summer)
  • Orographic (Relief) Rainfall: When moist winds rise over mountains — Western Ghats windward side, Himalayas foothills
  • Cyclonic Rainfall: Tropical cyclones in Bay of Bengal — affect Odisha, Andhra Pradesh, Tamil Nadu (Oct–Dec)
  • Western Disturbances: Winter rains in Punjab, Haryana, UP, Uttarakhand — beneficial for Rabi crops (wheat)
